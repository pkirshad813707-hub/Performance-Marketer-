Frequently Avoided Questions

Q: Do you actually know how to code?
A: Enough to be dangerous. Not enough to be trusted with production. I know the difference between padding and margin, which apparently makes me a god to some people.
Q: What's your favorite design trend?
A: Them disappearing. Trends are what insecure designers follow. Real taste is timeless disdain for everything.
Q: How long does a project take?
A: Officially? Whatever we agreed to. Realistically? 30% longer. I'm an optimist about deadlines, which is code for "bad at math."
Q: Will you work for exposure?
A: Only if you're literally dying and I'm your last hope. Even then, I'm charging hazard pay. Exposure doesn't pay my rent. My credit card company remains unimpressed.
Q: Can you make it "pop"?
A: That's either the dumbest or smartest brief I've heard, depending on what "it" is. Try being specific next time. I'm not a mind reader, I'm a designer.
Q: Do you do revisions?
A: Yes. Three rounds included. After that, it's blood money. At revision round 47, I start adding Comic Sans just to see if you're paying attention.
